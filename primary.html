Primary

Beaconsfield Elite Primary combines the best features of both British and Nigerian Curricula. Teaching children from five to eleven years of age, and with the British National Curriculum outlining what must be taught at each level and giving attainment targets, our teachers track each individual pupil’s ability early on, and then plan their learning curve accordingly.

Beaconsfield Elite Primary delivers the curriculum at age marked categories known as ‘Key Stages’. The National Curriculum in the UK defines the minimum educational requirements for students of compulsory school age (5–16years). It is organized on the basis of five Key Stages, and as an Early Years and Primary School our educational programme is completed at the end of Keystage2. At the end of each Key Stage, students take assessments to determine the levels they have reached.

The key stages are as follows: Key Stage 0: Nursery and reception years (3-5yearsold) .Now included as part of the Early Years Foundation StageKey Stage 1: Years 1 to 2 (5-7 years old)Key Stage 2: Years 3 to 6 (7-11yearsold)Primary school pupils in our key stages follow a programme of education in the subjects below:French, English language, Mathematics, Science,Information and Communication Technology, Design Technology, History, Geography, Art and Design, Music, Physical Education, Chess, Quantitative Reasoning, Verbal Aptitude, Citizenship Education and Agricultural science.

BEA Primary School starts from Year 1 through Year 6. Particular emphasis is placed on the developmental stages of growth at each level and the learning styles of the students. Teachers develop a program geared to those concerns as they provide a solid foundation in basic skills for the mastery of subject matter .Pupils are joyous learners as they are encouraged to integrate the material into their lives. Teachers and parents work closely together to help the pupils develop age-appropriate study and organizational skills. Pupils are encouraged to be independent and take responsibility for their learning.

BEA Primary School strives to help pupils share their unique gifts and to appreciate the gifts of others. Emphasis is placed on effective communication skills as they begin the foundation of lasting friendships.
